\title{Flow-Aware FiLM-Conditioned GNNs for Differentiated-QoS Online Routing via Deep Reinforcement Learning}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
    Modern backbone networks must simultaneously carry heterogeneous traffic ranging from latency-sensitive mice flows to throughput-hungry elephant flows. Existing online routing heuristics optimize for a single objective and are inherently myopic because they cannot foresee future arrivals; meanwhile, learning-based approaches rarely model how mixed QoS intents dynamically reshape the relevance of the same physical topology. We present a flow-conditional encoder, FiLM-GNN, which injects per-flow QoS intent into every message-passing layer of a Graph Neural Network (GNN) via Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM), causing identical substrate graphs to be embedded differently for heterogeneous flows. Formulating online per-flow routing as a stochastic MDP, we train the policy with PPO. Extensive experiments on the GEANT topology show that FiLM-GNN significantly outperforms OSPF, ECMP, and CSPF in cumulative utility, bandwidth satisfaction ratio, and end-to-end latency. Ablation studies confirm the necessity of FiLM conditioning and its operator-agnostic gains; interpretability analyses further verify that the policy causally splits routing decisions based on flow intent.

\end{abstract}
