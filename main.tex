\begin{abstract}
Modern backbone networks carry heterogeneous traffic: small-bandwidth flows (e.g., latency-sensitive control traffic, remote desktop) demand strict latency guarantees and full bandwidth satisfaction, whereas large-bandwidth flows (e.g., distributed AI training, bulk backup) seek to maximize throughput and tolerate best-effort service. Existing online routing heuristics are myopic, as they cannot foresee future arrivals, while prevailing learning-based methods produce a single topology embedding shared by all flows regardless of their QoS intent, which limits their ability to differentiate routing under mixed services. We present QoS-FiLM, an encoder that conditions every message-passing layer of a graph neural network (GNN) on the QoS intent of individual flows through Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM), so that an identical substrate graph is embedded differently for heterogeneous flows. We cast online per-flow routing as a stochastic Markov decision process and train the policy with proximal policy optimization (PPO). Across the GEANT, NOBEL-EU, and ABILENE topologies, QoS-FiLM surpasses both classical heuristics and learned ablative baselines in cumulative utility, bandwidth satisfaction ratio, and end-to-end latency. On GEANT, its mean reward improves by 0.167 over the strongest heuristic OnlineCSPF and by 0.219 over the no-FiLM variant noFiLM-Tf\textcolor{red}{这个方法是消融的方法，不是独立的learned baseline}, its bandwidth score reaches 0.759, and its average delay is reduced to 112.9 ms. Ablation studies confirm that FiLM conditioning is essential and that it interacts strongly with the attention operator, and interpretability analysis verifies that the encoder is highly sensitive to QoS intent while the policy discriminates routing decisions by flow type.
\end{abstract}
