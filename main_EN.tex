\begin{abstract}
Modern backbone networks carry heterogeneous traffic: small-bandwidth flows (e.g., latency-sensitive control signaling, remote desktop) demand strict latency guarantees and full bandwidth satisfaction, whereas large-bandwidth flows (e.g., distributed AI training, bulk backup) seek to maximize throughput and tolerate best-effort service. Existing online routing heuristics are myopic because they cannot foresee future arrivals. Prevailing learning-based methods share this limitation by producing a single topology embedding shared by all flows regardless of their QoS intent, which limits their ability to differentiate routing under mixed services. We present QoS-FiLM, a flow-conditioned graph architecture that uses Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM) to inject the QoS intent of each flow into every message-passing layer of a Graph Neural Network (GNN). This allows an identical substrate graph to be embedded differently for heterogeneous flows. We cast online per-flow routing as a stochastic Markov decision process and train the policy with proximal policy optimization (PPO). Across the GEANT, NOBEL-EU, and ABILENE topologies, QoS-FiLM surpasses both classical heuristics and learned network baselines in cumulative utility, bandwidth satisfaction ratio, and end-to-end latency. On GEANT, its mean reward improves by 0.167 over the strongest heuristic CSPF and by 0.219 over the flow-unaware ablation noFiLM-Tf; relative to noFiLM-Tf, the bandwidth score improves by 26.3\% and the average delay drops by 28.5\%. Ablation studies confirm that FiLM conditioning is essential and that it interacts strongly with the attention operator. Interpretability analysis further verifies that the encoder is highly sensitive to QoS intent and that the policy discriminates routing decisions by flow type.
\end{abstract}
